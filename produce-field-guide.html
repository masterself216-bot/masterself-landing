The Produce Field Guide

Two simple tools for your weekly plate: the brassica family — your metabolic and hormonal reset button — and the full-color rainbow rotation your coach references when building your meal plan.

The Brassica Family

Cruciferous vegetables. Aim for at least one serving daily — raw, steamed, or roasted.

🥦

Broccoli

Sulforaphane for detox support & cellular defense.

🥬

Kale

Dense in calcium, vitamin K, and lutein for eye health.

🧅

Cabbage

Gut-friendly fiber and natural anti-inflammatory compounds.

🌱

Brussels Sprouts

High protein-per-calorie for a vegetable, plus fiber.

🥦

Cauliflower

Low-carb bulk, choline for brain and liver function.

🌿

Bok Choy

Light, hydrating, easy to digest — great for evening meals.

🔴

Radish

Sharp, digestion-supportive, mineral-rich in small doses.

🥗

Arugula

Nitrates for blood flow and cardiovascular support.

Eat the Rainbow — Weekly Rotation

Each color group carries a distinct set of protective compounds. Rotate through all five across the week.

Red
ExamplesTomatoes, red peppers, strawberries, watermelon
SupportsHeart health, blood flow, lycopene protection
Orange & Yellow
ExamplesCarrots, sweet potato, squash, mango, citrus
SupportsVision, immune defense, skin repair
Green
ExamplesSpinach, kale, broccoli, green beans, avocado
SupportsDetox pathways, folate, cellular repair
Blue & Purple
ExamplesBlueberries, blackberries, purple cabbage, eggplant
SupportsBrain function, memory, anti-aging compounds
White & Tan
ExamplesGarlic, onion, mushroom, cauliflower, ginger
SupportsImmune signaling, circulation, gut microbiome

Target for the week: touch every color at least twice, and include one brassica serving daily. Your coach will reference this guide directly when building your personalized weekly meal plan — no need to track it perfectly on your own.
